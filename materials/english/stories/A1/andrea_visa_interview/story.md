# The Important Interview

Andrea is nervous. She has a visa interview next week. She wants to live in the UK with her husband, Tom. Tom is English. He lives in London.

Andrea visits her friend Sofia. Sofia passed her visa interview last year. She knows what questions they ask.

"What do they ask?" says Andrea.

"They ask many questions about your husband," says Sofia. "Do you know his birthday?"

Andrea thinks. "Yes! It is the fourteenth of March."

"Good. And what is his address in London?"

"He lives at twenty-three Baker Street, London." Andrea smiles.

"What is his job?" asks Sofia.

Andrea stops. "He works in a hospital. He is a nurse. But I don't know the name of the hospital."

"You need to know," says Sofia. "Call him tonight."

Andrea writes it in her notebook.

"What are his parents' names?" asks Sofia.

"His mother is Susan. His father is David." Andrea is happy. She knows this.

"How did you meet?" asks Sofia.

"We met online. We talked for six months. Then he came to visit me." Andrea smiles.

"How do you talk now? You live in different countries."

"We call every day on video. Sometimes we send messages."

"Good. Now — what are his hobbies?" says Sofia.

Andrea thinks. "He likes football. He watches it every weekend. And he loves to cook Italian food."

Sofia smiles. "You know a lot! But one more thing. How much money does he earn?"

Andrea is quiet. "I don't know exactly."

"You must find out," says Sofia. "The officer asks about money too."

Andrea picks up her phone. She calls Tom.

After the call, she writes the answer in her notebook. She reads all her notes again.

"Tom's birthday. His address. His job and his salary. His parents' names. How we met. How we talk. His hobbies." She closes the notebook.

"I am ready," says Andrea.

## Vocabulary

| Word | English |
|---|---|
| nervous | feeling worried or scared |
| visa interview | a meeting where an officer asks you questions |
| salary | the money someone earns from their job |
| hobbies | activities you like to do in your free time |
| officer | a person who works for the government |
| notebook | a small book you write notes in |

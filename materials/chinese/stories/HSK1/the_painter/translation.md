# The Painter's World

Li Ming is a painter. He loves colors very much. His home is full of colorful paintings: red flowers, orange trees, a yellow sun, green mountains, blue water, a purple sky. He also has white paper, black pens, pink flowers, a brown chair, a gray cat, and a golden cup. Li Ming is very happy, because he loves all these colors.

Every morning, Li Ming goes out to work. He is very quiet — he doesn't want anyone to see him. When he sees a gray wall, he paints it blue. When he sees a white door, he paints it green. When he sees a tree, he paints red, orange, and yellow flowers on it. When he sees a black chair, he paints it gold. When he sees a road, he paints it purple. He is very happy, because he is making the world more beautiful.

People notice and are delighted. They say: "The world is so beautiful! Who did this?" No one knows it was Li Ming. Everyone loves the colors, and everyone is happy.

But soon, many people begin to copy Li Ming. They too buy paints and start coloring the world. They paint the cars red, the buildings purple, the roads orange, the trees pink, the sky gold, the ground brown, the dogs blue, and the cats gray. The colors in the world grow more and more, more and more chaotic.

Every day Li Ming goes out and sees too many colors. He is no longer happy. He says: "This is no good! Too many colors. My eyes are tired. I want a quiet world."

Now, Li Ming only paints with black and white. He paints black trees on white paper, and white birds on black paper. His friend asks him: "Why don't you use colors anymore?" He says: "Colors are wonderful, but too many is not beautiful. Black and white are beautiful too."

His friends listen, think it over, and all come to understand: a world with too many colors is not beautiful; a world with no colors is not beautiful either. The most beautiful world is one with just a little color.
